ABOUT UDC

Who We Are

The Unmanned Aerial Systems Development Center (UDC) is a scientific association established in 2009 within the Aerospace Department at the Faculty of Engineering, Cairo University.

Vision

To become Egypt's premier UAS research and education center, connecting aerospace students, researchers and professionals to global innovation.

Mission

Fostering sustainable growth in aerospace through innovation, collaboration, and excellence. We empower our community with hands-on UAS education, serving national needs and developing the next generation of aerospace leaders.

Facilities

Fabrication Facilities

Equipped with 3D printers, various CNC machines, and composite manufacturing capabilities. UDC specializes in rapid prototyping and structural manufacturing — turning concept designs into flight-ready systems.

Testing & Validation Facilities

Comprehensive testing rigs covering structural, control, aerodynamic, and propulsion domains. Includes dedicated subsonic wind tunnel facilities and complete systems flight-testing capabilities.

UDC SUB-TEAMS

UDC consists of five specialized sub-teams collaborating to design, build, test, and fly our advanced unmanned aerial systems.

Vehicle Design

Responsible for overall aerodynamic configuration, sizing, performance estimation, and conceptual aircraft layout.

Mechanical Design

Focuses on CAD modeling, detailed structural components, stress analyses, internal layout configurations, and fabrication.

GN&C

Configures flight controllers, writes autopilot code, designs custom control systems, and manages sensor integration.

Piloting

Conducts simulation testing, manual flights of prototypes, flight checklist verification, and telemetry analysis.

Media, PR & FR

Drives brand communication, coordinates sponsorships, manages public events, social platforms, and document layouts.

Innovation in Action

GRADUATION PROJECTS

2025

Distributed Propulsion VTOL – Phase III

A modular "tail-sitter" VTOL UAV replacing mechanical control surfaces with differential thrust for stabilization and maneuvering.

  • Innovation: 41% weight reduction via composite layouts
  • Control: Differential thrust configuration
  • Validation: CFD simulations and wind tunnel tests
2025

UAV Medical Supplies Delivery System

Comprehensive logistics framework for medical deliveries across Egypt using a custom fixed-wing UAV platform.

  • Range: 31 km range with catapult launch
  • Efficiency: 50–90% reduction in delivery costs
  • Aero: Wind-tunnel optimized low-speed propulsion
2024

Aerial Archaeological Robot

Dual-robot system (tethered drone + ground rover) designed for exploring fragile historical and archaeological sites safely.

  • Sensors: 3D SLAM mapping via LiDAR & depth cameras
  • Power: DC-DC tether converters for unlimited flight
  • Autopilot: PX4 customized for complex tether dynamics
2023

Multi-Purpose Autonomous UAV – Phase II

Sizing and conceptualization of a multi-purpose aircraft intended for agriculture, precision mapping, and regional surveillance.

  • Wingspan: 1.65m span, 3.3kg Max Take-off Weight
  • Endurance: 60-minute flight time, 50km range
  • Payload: 700g configurable sensors

SIGNIFICANT RESEARCH

Selected PhD & Master Theses

2023
Obstacle Avoidance Algorithm for Quadcopter Platform

Ahmed Ali Elsayed Eltahan • Master Thesis

Developed and validated LiDAR-based obstacle avoidance using ROS and PX4. Demonstrated that VFH algorithms excelled in cluttered spaces while APF suited fast-response maneuvers.

2022
Flight Control System Design for Fault-Tolerant VTOL

Wessam Ahmed Abdelsabour • Master Thesis

Designed a VTOL UAV controller maintaining full trajectory control during motor failures. Enhances turn rates beyond traditional limits with active fault margin control.

2022
Aerodynamic Study of Aircraft with Distributed Electric Propulsion

Mohamed Ahmed Hetta • Master Thesis

Analyzed aerodynamic coupling between distributed electric thrusters and main wings. Validated lift enhancements and cruise efficiency at Cairo University wind tunnels.

2021
GPS-Denied Navigation using RNN and Inertial Sensors

Ahmed Ali Ahmed AbdulMajuid • PhD Research

Used Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) to correct low-cost IMU drift in GPS-denied environments. Reduced cumulative positioning error by 98% during 5-minute outages.

Our Heritage

UDC HISTORY

2006-2007

Pre-UDC Roots

Prior to UDC's establishment, the roots of the lab were already taking shape through two notable graduation projects.

  • Quad-Rotor Flying Robot (2006) — Early research on quadcopter flight mechanics and stabilization.
  • Phoenix 607 (2007) — Fixed-wing UAV testing and aerodynamic sizing.
2009

UDC Founded

The Unmanned Aerial Systems Development Center was officially established within the Aerospace Department at Cairo University.

  • Established under department supervision to foster student-led aerospace research and development.
  • Created a foundation for subsequent generations of UAV builders, academic researchers, and pilots.
2009-2010

Early Workshops

Glider workshops and Control Line workshops launch, introducing undergraduate students to hands-on aerospace education.

  • Glider Workshops — Taught fundamentals of aerodynamics, wing design, and stability through raw balsa prototypes.
  • Control Line Workshops — Provided hands-on experience building and flying tethered RC aircraft.
2010-2011

African Hawk Projects

African Hawk I and II graduation projects mark UDC's first flying wing unmanned aircraft, establishing iterative design.

  • First Flying Wing configurations designed and built using custom composite materials.
  • African Hawk I (2010) & African Hawk II (2011) — Set benchmarks for long-endurance and structural efficiency.
2012-2014

Growth & Competition Era

The growth continues through Mantis 18-J and AlQahira-400 graduation projects, along with internal RC challenges.

  • Mantis 18-J & AlQahira-400 (2012) — Conducted research on fixed-wing autopilots and real-time telemetry systems.
  • 4 Internal RC Competitions (2012-2014) — Incubated hands-on design and build culture among early undergraduate students.
2012-2015

Saqr Program

Saqr I and Saqr II projects push structural and aerodynamic design boundaries further with higher payloads.

  • Focused on composite reinforcement techniques for high-payload UAV frames.
  • Saqr I & Saqr II — Advanced autopilot integration, modular payload bay sizing, and flight stability analysis.
2014-2015

X-Platform I, II

The innovation continues through X-Platform I and X-Platform II summer campaigns, building critical skills.

  • ASTL Collaboration — Intensive summer camps in partnership with the Aerospace Systems Technology Laboratory.
  • Focused on multi-disciplinary engineering, autopilot coding, and advanced CNC/3D printing fabrication skills.
2015-2016

1st AIAA DBF Participation

UDC enters the AIAA Design/Build/Fly competition for the first time, beginning a streak of international participation.

  • Global Debut — Marked UDC's debut on the global stage, representing Cairo University in the United States.
  • Aviation Legacy — Established a long-term streak of international representation.
2017-Present

UDC-DBF Founded

UDC launches its own annual internal DBF competition, now training 150+ students per year in aerospace engineering.

  • Annual Challenge — Hands-on competition pushing 1st and 2nd year students to translate formulas to flying prototypes.
  • Mentorship Pipeline — Supervised by lab supervisors and mentors to ensure safety and design standards.
2006-Present

Research & Innovation Scale-Up

Since its roots, UDC has grown into Egypt's premier UAS student center, achieving high-impact research output.

  • Academic Excellence — Published peer-reviewed papers in AIAA Scitech, Ocean Engineering, and Springer.
  • Graduate Legacy — Sourced academic theses and talent pipeline supplying both local defense, commercial sector, and global aerospace firms.
